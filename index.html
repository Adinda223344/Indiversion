LIST PERFOMANCE

1 Tari Baksa Kembang

Tari Baksa Kembang is a traditional Banjar dance from South Kalimantan, Indonesia. It's a welcoming dance often performed by female dancers, typically in odd numbers, to greet guests, especially those of high standing. The dance depicts the beauty of flowers and the grace of young women in a garden.

2 Pakarena

Pakarena is a traditional court dance from the Makassar region of South Sulawesi, Indonesia. It's known for its slow, graceful movements performed by women, often accompanied by energetic drumming. While it originated in the court, it's now a popular form of entertainment and is used in various ceremonies.

3 Cakalele

Cakalele is a traditional Indonesian war dance originating from North and Central Maluku. It is performed by male dancers, often with two representing opposing leaders or captains, accompanied by warriors. The dance is known for its dynamic movements, including jumping, turning, stomping, and sword-slashing, reflecting its historical significance in warfare. While traditionally a war dance, in some regions, like Banda, it also incorporates Islamic religious and ethical values.

4 Tari TorTor

Tari Tor Tor is a traditional Batak dance from North Sumatra, Indonesia, often performed at various ceremonies and events. It's not just a dance, but a form of communication and expression, reflecting cultural and spiritual values. The dance is accompanied by gondang music, and each movement holds specific meanings, sometimes conveying hopes, prayers, or even addressing specific situations.

5 Angklung Arumbaraya

Arumba is the name of a form of musical performance that uses various traditional bamboo musical instruments, where the angklung is one of the main musical instruments. Arumba can also refer to a music group or orchestra that specializes in playing bamboo musical instruments.

6 Tari Topeng X Kuda Lumping
